# Use Intrinsic Motivation instead of Willpower

[Video](https://youtu.be/hQRjSn7_BKY)

## Action Crises

Analysis paralysis, it looks like but it is a conflict between what you want and what you should.
When there is a conflict, willpower or discipline is required to move from the default (**want**) to what we **should**.
`Autonomous Motivation` or #intrinsic_motivation(What you naturally want to do) and `Controlled Motivation` or #extrinsic_motivation(That needs willpower).

If you expend willpower, you need to expend more willpower in the future.
This is the common experience of a lot of people.
If you force yourself to do the shoulds, you are actually also suppressing your wants ie. Autonomous Motivation or Intrinsic Motivation.
Research shows that when you use willpower, after some time frustrations and setbacks set in and it becomes difficult to sustain.
I.e. When it becomes hard, it is more likely that you will do what you feel like.

When you force yourself, we are not overcoming but creating analysis paralysis.
When you force yourself to do something, the more you have to force yourself to do something.

Exception: Conscientiousness - some people are ok with expending willpower for long term goal without giving into desire. (those who can delay gratification)

Willpower becomes a drug that the motivation circuitry is addicted to and needs more and more of. 

`Willpower is a risk factor for action crises.`

## How to escape willpower trap

Habit is about automatically doing things. 
It completely bypasses the motivational circuitry.
Habit raises the floor (going 0 to 50), but exceptional results come when utilizing the motivational circuits using our intrinsic motivation.

Autonomous goals have the following effect:
- Consistency and Sustained goal effort
- Increased goal progress
- Reduced temptation (makes you want to put down the phone)

## Making a plan

A lot of times we make a plan, but dont implement things as much.
To have sustained progress, we need to cultivate Autonomous motivation.

Achieving progress does not happen from willpower, discipline or habit, it happens by cultivating internal drive this is where the questions come up.

```
What do you really want?
Why do you want it?
What makes this important to you?
```

## Action vs State
People think in either **Action** or **State**.

**Action**: what they want to do.
In this case the action is the end goal.

**State**: They dont think *what to do* but rather *what they have to do to feel some way*.
In this case the action is in service to the feeling.

Eg:
- How much do I have to work to feel relaxed => chasing Relaxed State
- How much I have to listen someone complain until I feel peace => Chasing Peaceful State
- Chasing euphoria through THC

This leads to more questions related to behavioral change #behavioral_change.
